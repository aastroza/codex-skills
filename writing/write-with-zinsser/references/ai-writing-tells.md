# AI Writing Tells

Use this checklist during the last editing pass. It adapts the public guidance in Wikipedia's "Signs of AI writing" page and the Isaac Flath starter wiki style notes.

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Signs_of_AI_writing

## Word clusters to distrust

Watch for dense clusters of these words. One word can be fine. Many of them in one passage usually means the prose has drifted into generic AI style.

- additionally
- align with
- boasts
- bolstered
- crucial
- delve
- emphasizing
- enduring
- enhance
- fostering
- garner
- highlight as a verb
- interplay
- intricate, intricacies
- key as a filler adjective
- landscape as an abstract noun
- meticulous, meticulously
- pivotal
- robust
- showcase
- tapestry as an abstract noun
- testament
- underscore as a verb
- valuable
- vibrant

Prefer plain replacements:

- additionally -> also, or cut it
- delve into -> examine, explain, or name the action
- enhance -> improve, sharpen, speed up, reduce, clarify
- showcase -> show
- underscore -> show, prove, make clear
- crucial, pivotal, key -> name why it matters or cut it
- landscape, tapestry, interplay -> name the actual field, system, or relationship

## Sentence patterns

Cut corrective constructions:

- not only X, but also Y
- not just X, but Y
- it is not X, it is Y
- no X, no Y, just Z

Say the positive claim directly.

Watch for fake contrast:

- "The issue is not sourcing. It is framing."
- "This is not a tool. It is a workflow."

These can work in human prose, but AI overuses them for emphasis. Keep only when the contrast is the actual argument.

## Grammar and diction

- Do not avoid "is", "are", or "has" just to sound polished.
- Prefer "is" over "serves as", "stands as", "marks", or "represents" when identity is all you mean.
- Prefer "has" over "boasts", "features", "offers", or "maintains" when possession is all you mean.
- Reuse the accurate noun. Do not rotate through synonyms to avoid repetition.
- Do not inflate a simple fact with notability language, media language, or attribution theater.

## Markdown and formatting tells

- Avoid title case headings unless the project style requires it.
- Avoid heavy boldface. Bold should help a reader scan, not decorate the page.
- Avoid bullet lists where each item starts with a bold inline header and a colon.
- Avoid thematic breaks before every heading.
- Do not skip heading levels.
- Use prose when a tiny table adds nothing.
- Use straight quotes unless the target publication requires curly quotes.
- Avoid em dashes.

## Rule of three

AI often stacks three adjectives, three nouns, or three short phrases to imply completeness.

Examples to question:

- "clear, practical, and actionable"
- "students, teachers, and administrators"
- "build, deploy, and scale"

The pattern is not banned. Cut it when the third item exists only for rhythm.

## Final check

Ask these questions:

1. Would a person say this sentence across a table?
2. Does this paragraph contain a specific observation, source detail, or choice?
3. Did the draft use structure to help the reader, or to imitate polish?
4. Did the ending land on a real insight, or a neat wrap-up?
